# V21.6 Calculation Change Impact

The figures below compare the same representative scenarios in the untouched V21.5 baseline and V21.6. They demonstrate the expected direction of change; they are not a replacement for site-specific reruns.

## Default representative case at 8% discount rate

| Metric | V21.5 | V21.6 | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Initial operator investment | €205,044 | €205,044 | No grant or deployment change |
| 20-year NPV | €1,665,228 | €1,422,894 | More conservative from correct timing, reliability and OPEX treatment |
| IRR | 57.00% | 41.51% | Corrected project cashflow convention and solver |
| Net ROI | 19.38x | 18.15x | Updated served energy and cost treatment |
| Payback | Year 4 marker | 3.40 years | Fractional payback |
| Year-1 delivered kWh | 118,225 | 114,087 | Reliability availability is now active |
| Lease-secured NPV | Not shown | €1,113,879 | Separates secured return from post-lease value |
| Post-lease undiscounted cashflow | Not shown | €1,206,632 | Explicit exposure beyond lease term |

## Grant stress case: €250,000 requested

| Metric | V21.5 | V21.6 |
|---|---:|---:|
| Gross initial CAPEX | Not retained separately | €205,044 |
| Net initial investment | **–€44,956** | **€0** |
| Grant applied | €250,000 implicit | €205,044 |
| Unapplied grant | Not shown | €44,956 |
| IRR | Invalid 1,000% | No valid IRR |

V21.6 prevents an excess grant from becoming an artificial day-one cash inflow.

## Actual installed CAPEX case: €865,368

| Metric | V21.5 | V21.6 |
|---|---:|---:|
| Battery deployment treatment | Staged | Installed at COD |
| Gross lifecycle CAPEX | €1,190,063 | €1,134,208 |
| NPV at 8% | €1,013,853 | €764,176 |
| IRR | 18.39% | 15.22% |
| Payback | Year 8 marker | 7.58 years |

V21.6 recognises that an actual-built project CAPEX already represents installed day-one assets, eliminating the duplicate later battery purchase while also applying corrected timing and operating assumptions.

## Interpretation

The corrected model will often produce lower NPV and IRR than V21.5. This is intentional: the release removes early cashflow timing bias, invalid IRRs, inactive reliability assumptions and inconsistent asset/OPEX treatment. It does not reduce the central demand funnel simply to force conservative outputs.
