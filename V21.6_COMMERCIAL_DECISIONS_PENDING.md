# V21.6 Commercial Decisions Pending

The following items were analysed but deliberately not activated automatically because they change investment policy or forecast assumptions rather than correcting engineering defects.

## 1. Segment calibration of new-site demand

V21.6 retains the approved site-type demand profiles. A future calibration should fit composite conversion factors against clean operating histories, report sample sizes and out-of-sample error, and obtain commercial sign-off before activation.

## 2. Default discount or hurdle rate

The model now warns when NPV is shown at 0%. Management should approve the default discount rate and whether it varies by project risk, funding structure or lease length.

## 3. Scenario recommendation objective

The existing feasibility-first and ROI-based order is retained. Management should decide whether the primary recommendation should instead be based on NPV, secured-lease NPV, IRR, payback or a weighted/risk-adjusted score.

## 4. New-site downside/base/upside assumptions

Operating-site P25/P50/P75 bands are now surfaced from the maturity engine. New/unbuilt sites require a separately approved uncertainty framework covering demand conversion, BEV uptake, session energy, price, electricity cost and CAPEX.

## 5. Post-lease valuation policy

V21.6 shows secured-lease and post-lease economics separately. Management should decide whether post-lease cashflows are excluded, probability-weighted or included in the headline investment recommendation.

## 6. Battery service commercial source

V21.6 uses the technical-engine site-level battery service value as the authoritative OPEX source to eliminate conflicting formulas. Confirm this against current supplier/service contracts before the next commercial baseline update.
