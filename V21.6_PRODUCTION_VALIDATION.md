## No-lookahead rolling validation

The figures below were generated with rolling historical forecast origins. Every historical forecast used only information available at its origin, and method performance was assessed leave-one-site-out.

| Horizon | Forecast origins | Unique sites | WAPE | Portfolio bias | P80 absolute percentage error | Selected global blend |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| 3 months | 101 | 22 | 17.0% | −8.6% | 29.0% | 75% recent-30 / 25% maturity-ramp |
| 6 months | 57 | 11 | 23.8% | −9.1% | 44.2% | 25% recent-30 / 75% maturity-ramp |
| 12 months | 10 | 5 | 29.4% | −15.0% | 57.6% | 50% recent-30 / 50% maturity-ramp |

Negative bias means the validation forecasts remained conservative on aggregate.

The engine does not necessarily use these global weights for every site. It uses the closest sufficiently supported horizon/age/category profile and falls back conservatively where evidence is limited.

## Known limitations and honest interpretation

- Twelve-month validation currently contains only five independent sites and ten forecast origins. It is useful evidence, but not a large enough sample for a strong universal accuracy claim.
- Young and ramping sites retain materially wider uncertainty than repeatable mature sites.
- The empirical maturity curve is trained from four stable active-site profiles in this dataset; it will become more reliable as additional mature sites and time periods are added.
- Disruption detection can identify suspicious patterns but cannot determine the operational cause without uptime, maintenance or incident data.
- A model snapshot created now is a first-available baseline unless the original approval model was actually preserved at the approval date.
- External map and font network services were not fully testable inside the sandbox; local application logic and fallback behaviour were tested.

## Result

**PASS** — V21.6 preserves the approved V21.5 visual baseline and passes the complete regression suite. The prediction engine is materially more evidence-led and auditable, while remaining transparent about the limited twelve-month sample and the uncertainty of young-site forecasts.
